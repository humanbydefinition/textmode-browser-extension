# Contributor Covenant 3.0 Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make this project community welcoming, safe, and equitable for everyone who participates in good
faith.

We are committed to an environment that respects the dignity, rights, and contributions of all people,
regardless of race, ethnicity, caste, color, age, physical characteristics, neurodiversity, disability, sex or
gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, language, philosophy or religion, national or social
origin, socio-economic position, education level, experience level, or other status.

## Encouraged Behaviors

We understand that words and actions may be interpreted differently across cultures, backgrounds, and native
languages. We agree to participate with care and to support the purpose of the project.

Encouraged behavior includes:

1. Respecting the purpose of the community and the spaces where we collaborate.
2. Engaging kindly, honestly, and constructively.
3. Respecting different viewpoints, experiences, constraints, and skill levels.
4. Taking responsibility for our words, actions, and contributions.
5. Giving and receiving constructive feedback with care.
6. Repairing harm when mistakes happen.
7. Helping sustain a healthy community for users, contributors, and maintainers.

## Restricted Behaviors

The following behaviors are not acceptable in project spaces. Threats, encouragement, or promotion of these
behaviors are also violations.

1. Harassment, including repeated personal attention after a clear request to stop.
2. Insults, demeaning comments, or character attacks directed at a person or group.
3. Stereotyping, discrimination, or exclusion based on identity or personal characteristics.
4. Sexualized language, imagery, attention, or conduct that is inappropriate for the project context.
5. Sharing or using private information without explicit permission.
6. Threatening, encouraging, or causing violence or other harm.
7. Behavior that reasonably threatens the safety, participation, or well-being of the community.

Additional restricted behavior includes:

1. Impersonating another person or evading moderation actions through misleading identity.
2. Failing to credit sources for contributed content, examples, code, assets, or documentation.
3. Posting promotional or commercial material outside the norms of the project.
4. Linking to, describing, or presenting restricted content irresponsibly.

## Reporting an Issue

Conflict can happen even when people are trying to collaborate in good faith. Not every disagreement is a code
of conduct violation, but reports are taken seriously when community safety or participation is at risk.

To report a possible violation, contact the maintainers at
[hello@textmode.art](mailto:hello@textmode.art).

Please include what happened, where it happened, who was involved, and any links, screenshots, logs, or context
that can help the maintainers understand the report. Do not investigate harassment yourself or publicly post
private details about an incident.

Maintainers will review reports as promptly and fairly as possible. They will prioritize reporter privacy and
community safety, and they will keep enforcement details private when public disclosure would create additional
harm.

## Addressing and Repairing Harm

Maintainers may use the following enforcement ladder to determine an appropriate response. Depending on the
severity and impact of an incident, lower steps may be skipped.

1. Warning
    - Event: A single violation or a small series of violations.
    - Consequence: A private written warning from maintainers.
    - Repair: The person may be asked to acknowledge impact, apologize, clarify intent, or adjust behavior.
2. Temporarily Limited Activities
    - Event: A repeated violation after a warning, or a first violation with more serious impact.
    - Consequence: A written warning with a time-limited restriction on specific spaces, conversations, or
      interactions.
    - Repair: The person may be asked to use the cooldown period to reflect and to follow conditions for
      returning to normal participation.
3. Temporary Suspension
    - Event: A repeated pattern that warnings did not resolve, or a serious single violation.
    - Consequence: Temporary removal from community spaces, tools, or communication channels with conditions for
      return.
    - Repair: The person must respect the suspension and meet the stated conditions before returning.
4. Permanent Ban
    - Event: A pattern of violations that other steps failed to resolve, or a violation serious enough that the
      maintainers cannot keep the community safe while the person remains present.
    - Consequence: Removal from project spaces, tools, and communication channels.
    - Repair: No return path is expected for cases of this severity.

This ladder is a guideline. Maintainers may use judgment when needed to protect the community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews,
commits, documentation, release channels, community chat, and project events.

It also applies when someone officially represents the project in public spaces, such as using an official email
address, posting from an official account, presenting the project at an event, or acting as an appointed project
representative.
